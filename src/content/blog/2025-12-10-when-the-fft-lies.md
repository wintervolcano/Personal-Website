title: When the FFT lies
date: 2025-12-10
description: Peaks are not truth. They're hints — and sometimes they're decoys.
tags: [fft, signal-processing]
---

A tall peak is satisfying, but it isn't automatically a pulsar.

- Windowing, red noise, and RFI can all create convincing structure.
- Harmonics can dominate the fundamental.
- Small changes in preprocessing can shuffle the peak ranking.

Search Mode on this site exaggerates the idea: there are *decoys* on purpose — so your brain practices skepticism.
